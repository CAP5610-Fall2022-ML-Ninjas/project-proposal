Background¶

Research at University of Colorado Boulder highlighted and quantified stark inequalities

  • Faculty production
  • Prestige - 20% tenure-track professors trained at top 5 US universities
  • Retention - higher attrition rates among faculty trained outside the United States
  • Gender - gains from past decade unlikely to lead to long-term gender parity in most fields

Faculty hiring / retention impact-

  • Development and spread of ideas and research priorities
  • Societal and academic priorities
  • Careers / Educational outcomes
  • Generational turnover and efforts to diversify the professoriate
    • Gender
    • Race
    • Socioeconomic origin

Problem Statement¶

Develop a supervised learning model that can predict a professor's success based on their background, socioeconomic status, and affiliations.

  • Study statistically analyzed the data
  • Did not apply ML algorithms for forecasting

Essential Tasks: Clustering + Ranking

Relevance¶

Very thorough analysis of tenured or tenure-track faculty-

  • Consistent bias has a negative impact on high-performing professors
  • Limited by circumstances and connections

Our model will-

  • Predict the prevalance of those biases
  • Generate additional insights to support the study

Data Collection Requirements¶

Our project relies on specific data about US faculty and their career path.

  • Request data through AARC [aarcresearch.com]
  • Search for alternate data sources

Data Pre-processing Steps¶

  1. Remove unidentifiable and duplicate university entries
  2. Standardize the degrees, e.g. PhD == DPhil
  3. Remove duplicate departments
  4. Classify each department in a two level taxonomy: domain -> field
  5. Remove inconsistent employment records, e.g. faculty employed at different universities in same year
  6. Fill missing employment record, e.g. sabbatical leave
  7. Remove non-primary appointments, e.g. courtesy/emeritus appointments with multiple departments
  8. Handle employment records with mid-career moves
  9. Remove inconsistently sampled departments

Quality of Data for ML Models¶

  • Source data high quality
    • Pre-processed from raw census data
  • Able to reproduce study results
  • Not ready for ML models
